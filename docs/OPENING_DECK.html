What is vibe coding?

You describe it. The AI builds it. You say what's wrong. Repeat.

1
You tell Claude what you want - what it should do, who it's for - in plain English. No syntax, no setup.
2
Claude writes the code and you get a working thing on screen. Not a mock-up. Software that runs.
3
You test it and react. "That button's in the wrong place." "It should sort by date." Keep going until it does what you imagined.
→
The skill isn't programming. It's thinking clearly about a problem and saying what you mean. Lawyers are good at that.

The shift
Turning "I've got an idea" into "here's a working version" used to take months, a budget and a business case. Now it can take an afternoon.
That changes what's worth trying. The expensive part used to be building. Increasingly, the scarce thing is knowing what to build - and that's a judgement you're already developing.

What makes a good idea

Real. Specific. Contained.

R
Real - something you've actually experienced, not a theory about "legal inefficiency".
S
Specific - "trainees waste time chasing signature pages" beats "law is slow". Narrow enough to build in an hour.
C
Contained - one focused tool, not a platform. Lean smaller than feels comfortable.
Document tasks

Signature pages, redline summaries, CP lists, first-pass checks.

Training & learning

Tools that help trainees get up to speed in a new seat faster.

Relationships & BD

Staying close to clients, spotting opportunities, prepping for the conversation.

Knowledge

Making the firm's expertise easier to find and reuse.

How the next 80 minutes work

Build at your table. Then a few groups show the room.

60 min
Build session - at your table with an Innovation Team facilitator. First 10 min: work out what you're building. Then kick off and build. Last 10 min: polish and lock the demo. You don't touch a keyboard unless you want to - the facilitator drives, you do the thinking.
20 min
Present back - three to five groups show what they built: the problem, a live demo, what it'd take to ship, the ask. Build something you'd be happy to show even if you're not picked.
Your day-plan handout has the detail. Don't worry about the code - that's not the point.
