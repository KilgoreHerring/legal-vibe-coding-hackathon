# Discovery Questions

Work through these as a group before you ask Claude to build anything. The better your answers, the better your app. Your facilitator will guide you.

Budget 15 minutes. If you're still debating after 15 minutes, pick something and move.

---

## 1. The Problem

- What is the specific problem you're solving? (Be precise - "lawyers waste time on X" is better than "law is slow")
- Who experiences this problem? (Trainee? Associate? Client? Secretary?)
- How is it solved today? (Email chain? Manual spreadsheet? It just isn't solved?)
- How much time or effort does the current approach waste?

---

## 2. The User

- Who is the primary user of your app?
- What does their day look like? When would they use this?
- What do they already know how to do? What would confuse them?
- What would make them love this vs. just tolerate it?

---

## 3. The Solution

- In one sentence, what does your app do?
- What are the 2–3 core things it must do? (Your MVP - minimum viable product)
- What would be nice to have but isn't essential for the demo?
- What does it NOT do? (Scope control is a skill)

---

## 4. The Data

- What information does the app need to work?
- Where does that information come from? (User input? A document? An existing system?)
- Are there any real data sources you could connect to later?
- Does the app handle any sensitive or confidential information?

---

## 5. The Demo

- What does success look like at the end of 90 minutes?
- What will you show the other groups?
- What's the one thing you want them to walk away remembering?

---
