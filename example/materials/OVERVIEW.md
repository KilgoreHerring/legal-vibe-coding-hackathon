# Litigation Training Exercise — Fairway Events Ltd v Luxe Marquees Ltd

## Overview

A realistic pre-action litigation exercise for trainees in a Disputes seat. Trainees are given a full factual briefing, the other side's Letter Before Action, and the underlying contract. They must produce two documents: a protocol-compliant LBA and a privileged client advice note.

The exercise tests whether trainees understand the distinction between what goes in correspondence to the other side (firm assertion of position, no strategy) and what goes in privileged advice to the client (candid assessment of strengths AND weaknesses).

**Total marks available:** 85 (45 for LBA, 40 for Advice Note)

**Estimated time:** 5–7 hours (full exercise)

---

## The Scenario

Fairway Events Ltd (client) hired Luxe Marquees Ltd to supply and install a premium marquee, furniture and lighting for a major corporate gala for Harringtons plc on 19 July 2025. Luxe's performance was seriously deficient — late installation, wrong equipment delivered, and defective workmanship.

Luxe's solicitors (Barker & Co) have sent a Letter Before Action claiming the outstanding balance of £40,320. The client disputes the claim and wants to counterclaim for their losses. The client failed to respond to the October 2025 LBA — the trainee must address this.

---

## The Trainee's Role

The trainee is acting for **Fairway Events Ltd**. Their supervising partner is **Catherine Osei**.

They must:
1. Review all three documents
2. Draft a Letter Before Action to Barker & Co (response + defence + counterclaim)
3. Draft a Client Advice Note to Rebecca Haines (privileged, candid, balanced)

## Key Areas of Law

- Contract law — breach, repudiation, remoteness (*Hadley v Baxendale*), mitigation
- Liquidated damages vs penalties (*Makdessi*)
- Pre-Action Protocol for Debt Claims
- Practice Direction — Pre-Action Conduct and Protocols
- ADR and costs consequences (*Halsey v Milton Keynes*)
- Limitation (Limitation Act 1980)
- Indemnity and contribution (Civil Liability (Contribution) Act 1978)
