# Task 2 — Draft Client Advice Note

**Points available:** 40
**Type:** Privileged — legal advice to client (NOT seen by the other side)

---

## Your Task

Prepare a **Client Advice Note** for **Rebecca Haines, Operations Director, Fairway Events Ltd**.

---

## What the Advice Note Must Cover

### Assessment of Each Head of Claim

For each head, assess: **strengths, weaknesses and realistic prospects of recovery.**

- [ ] **Defence to Luxe's claim** — explain set-off, assess as strong
- [ ] **Liquidated damages** — assess as very strong, address potential penalty argument under *Makdessi*
- [ ] **Replacement furniture** — strong direct loss, address mitigation argument
- [ ] **Replacement lighting** — same analysis
- [ ] **Staff overtime** — assess reasonableness, note potential scrutiny of hours
- [ ] **Deposit recovery** — identify as **weakest head**, analyse total failure of consideration vs repudiatory breach, acknowledge affirmation risk (event went ahead), give realistic prospects
- [ ] **Harringtons loss** — CRITICAL: remoteness (*Hadley v Baxendale* two-limb test), **revenue vs profit** (must deduct costs of performing), mitigation, causation, realistic recovery range
- [ ] **Guest who tripped** — third-party liability risk, Clause 6.1 indemnity, contribution under Civil Liability (Contribution) Act 1978, recommend insurer notification

### Procedural Advice
- [ ] **Limitation** — confirm well within 6 years (breach July 2025, expiry July 2031)
- [ ] **Court and track allocation** — fast track vs multi-track; note multi-track if Harringtons loss included
- [ ] **Pre-action protocol** — address delay risk, reassure client, explain what to say if court asks
- [ ] **ADR recommendation** — recommend mediation, explain benefits, reference costs consequences
- [ ] **Costs** — explain standard basis recovery and the shortfall risk

### Overall Assessment
- [ ] **Best-case, realistic and worst-case scenarios** with figures
- [ ] **Settlement recommendation** with a range
- [ ] **Practical next steps** — what you need from the client (profit margin data, comparative quote, guest contact status, mitigation evidence)

---

## Important

> **This is privileged advice. Be candid and balanced — identify weaknesses honestly. The client wants a realistic assessment, not cheerleading.**
