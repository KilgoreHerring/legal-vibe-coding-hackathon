What We Built

A realistic litigation exercise
with instant AI grading

Realistic scenario

A fully-fleshed B2B dispute: Fairway Events v Luxe Marquees. Three real documents provided: client instructions, the opponent's LBA, and the underlying contract.

Guided three-step exercise

Step 1: review the documents. Step 2: draft a 45-point Letter Before Action. Step 3: draft a 40-point privileged Client Advice Note.

Rubric-based AI feedback

Instant grading against a real marking rubric: score, grade, per-criterion annotations, strengths, and specific improvements. Revise and resubmit as many times as you like.

Ask Catherine Osei

A floating chat panel powered by an AI supervising partner. Socratic, not prescriptive: she asks questions back rather than giving answers, just like a real supervisor would.

See It In Action

Meet Alex — first-year trainee,
Disputes seat, day one

The scenario

Alex has just received an email from Catherine Osei (partner): a client (Fairway Events) has had a marquee supplier disaster. There is an outstanding LBA from the other side and a client who wants to fight back. Catherine needs a response LBA and an advice note by end of day. Alex has never done this before.

→
Step 1: Alex opens the app, reads the client email, the opponent's LBA, and the contract. Clause tags highlight the key provisions.
→
Step 2: Alex drafts the LBA and asks Catherine about the liquidated damages clause. She replies: "What does Clause 5.2 say about whether that's a penalty?"
→
Step 3: Both drafts are submitted and graded. 31/45 on the LBA (missed the Harringtons remoteness issue), 28/40 on the advice note. Alex revises, resubmits, improves.

From Prototype to Production

What would it take
to actually ship this?

☁️
Azure deployment and firm security
The app would live inside the firm's Azure tenant, deployed via Azure Static Web Apps. Claude is available through Azure AI Foundry (Anthropic's enterprise offering on Azure), meaning all API calls stay within the firm's infrastructure, governed by existing data loss prevention and information security policies. Authentication via Microsoft Entra ID: no new logins, no separate credentials.
📋
Scenario library and partner sign-off
The rubric and model answer need validation by a litigation partner before trainees are graded against them. Extending to other practice areas (corporate, real estate, employment) requires new scenarios, built collaboratively with supervisors in each seat.
📊
Progress tracking and L&D integration
Scores currently live only in the browser session. Connecting to Azure Cosmos DB or an existing HR/L&D system would let supervisors see where trainees are struggling across cohorts and tailor supervision accordingly.
