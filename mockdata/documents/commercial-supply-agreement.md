# SUPPLY AGREEMENT (extract)

**Customer:** Brindle Foods plc ("**Customer**")
**Supplier:** Larchwood Cold Stores Limited ("**Supplier**")
**Date:** 2024-11-12
**Term:** 5 years from the Commencement Date, with rolling 24-month renewals unless terminated on 12 months' written notice.

> *This is an extract of the operative commercial clauses. Schedules and execution pages omitted.*

## 1. SUPPLY OF SERVICES

1.1 The Supplier shall provide cold-storage and onward distribution services in accordance with the Service Levels set out in Schedule 2 (the "**Services**").

1.2 The Supplier shall provide the Services with reasonable skill and care and using personnel of suitable qualification and experience.

## 2. SERVICE LEVELS AND CREDITS

2.1 The Supplier shall meet or exceed the Service Levels set out in Schedule 2. Failure to meet a Service Level shall entitle the Customer to Service Credits as set out in Schedule 2.

2.2 Service Credits are the Customer's sole financial remedy for failure to meet a Service Level, unless and until the failures constitute Persistent Failure (Schedule 2 paragraph 6) at which point the Customer may exercise its right to terminate under clause 11.3.

## 3. CHARGES AND PAYMENT

3.1 The Customer shall pay the Charges set out in Schedule 3.

3.2 Charges shall be reviewed annually on each anniversary of the Commencement Date by reference to the Consumer Prices Index, capped at 3% per annum.

3.3 Invoices shall be paid within **45 days** of receipt of a valid invoice. Late payment interest accrues at 4% above the Bank of England base rate (statutory rate).

## 4. WARRANTIES

4.1 Each party warrants that:

   (a) it has full power and authority to enter into and perform this Agreement;
   (b) its entry into and performance of this Agreement will not breach any other obligation owed by it.

4.2 The Supplier additionally warrants that:

   (a) the Services shall comply with all applicable laws and food safety regulations;
   (b) the Supplier shall maintain at all times the licences, permits and authorisations required to provide the Services;
   (c) goods stored shall be maintained at the temperatures and humidity ranges specified in Schedule 2.

## 5. INDEMNITY

5.1 The Supplier shall indemnify the Customer against all losses, damages, costs and expenses (including reasonable legal fees) incurred by the Customer arising out of or in connection with:

   (a) any breach by the Supplier of its warranties in clause 4.2;
   (b) any negligent act or omission of the Supplier or its personnel;
   (c) any death or personal injury caused by the Supplier or its personnel;
   (d) any infringement of third-party Intellectual Property Rights by the Services.

## 6. LIMITATION OF LIABILITY

6.1 Nothing in this Agreement limits or excludes either party's liability for:

   (a) death or personal injury caused by negligence;
   (b) fraud or fraudulent misrepresentation;
   (c) any matter where liability cannot be limited or excluded by law.

6.2 Subject to clause 6.1, neither party shall be liable for:

   (a) loss of profits, revenue or anticipated savings;
   (b) loss of business or goodwill;
   (c) any indirect, special or consequential losses.

6.3 Subject to clauses 6.1 and 6.2, the Supplier's total aggregate liability arising out of or in connection with this Agreement shall not exceed **125% of the Charges** paid or payable in the 12 months preceding the event giving rise to liability.

## 7. INSURANCE

7.1 The Supplier shall maintain at all times during the Term:

   (a) public and product liability insurance of not less than £10,000,000 per claim;
   (b) employer's liability insurance of not less than £10,000,000;
   (c) professional indemnity insurance of not less than £5,000,000 in aggregate.

## 8. DATA PROTECTION

8.1 Where the Supplier processes personal data on behalf of the Customer, the data processing terms set out in Schedule 4 (which incorporate the UK International Data Transfer Addendum) shall apply.

## 9. CONFIDENTIALITY

9.1 Each party shall keep confidential and not disclose any Confidential Information of the other party except as expressly permitted by this Agreement.

## 10. CHANGE OF CONTROL

10.1 Either party may terminate this Agreement on **30 days' written notice** if a Change of Control of the other party occurs. "**Change of Control**" has the meaning given in section 1124 of the Corporation Tax Act 2010.

## 11. TERMINATION

11.1 Either party may terminate this Agreement on 12 months' written notice expiring on or after the fifth anniversary of the Commencement Date.

11.2 Either party may terminate immediately on written notice if the other party:

   (a) commits a material breach which is incapable of remedy;
   (b) commits a material breach which is capable of remedy and fails to remedy it within 30 days of written notice;
   (c) suffers an Insolvency Event.

11.3 The Customer may terminate immediately on written notice in the case of Persistent Failure of Service Levels (Schedule 2 paragraph 6).

11.4 The Customer may terminate for convenience on 12 months' written notice. On any termination by the Customer for convenience, the Customer shall pay an Early Termination Fee equal to 30% of the average annual Charges over the previous three years.

## 12. EXIT AND TRANSITION

12.1 On termination or expiry, the Supplier shall provide reasonable transition assistance for up to 6 months at the Supplier's then current rates and shall return all Customer Materials and Customer Data within 30 days.

## 13. DISPUTE RESOLUTION

13.1 The parties shall first attempt to resolve any dispute by discussion between senior representatives. If unresolved within 30 days, the dispute may be referred to mediation under the CEDR Model Mediation Procedure.

## 14. GOVERNING LAW

14.1 This Agreement shall be governed by the law of England and Wales. The courts of England and Wales shall have exclusive jurisdiction.
