# CONSULTANCY AGREEMENT

**Client:** Mercer Coaching Group Ltd (registered number 09221176) (the "**Client**")
**Consultant:** Hartwell Performance Ltd (registered number 13884521) (the "**Consultant**")
**Personnel:** James Hartwell (the "**Key Person**")
**Date:** 2026-03-10

## 1. APPOINTMENT AND TERM

1.1 The Client engages the Consultant to provide the services described in **Schedule 1 (Services)** during the Engagement Period.

1.2 The **Engagement Period** begins on **1 April 2026** and continues for an initial period of **12 months**, unless terminated earlier in accordance with this Agreement. Thereafter the Engagement Period continues on a rolling basis until terminated on the notice in clause 9.

## 2. THE SERVICES

2.1 The Services are:

   (a) provision of strategic performance advice to the Client's senior coaching team;
   (b) design and delivery of a performance analytics framework;
   (c) attendance at up to four "performance review" workshops per quarter;
   (d) such other services as the parties may agree in writing.

2.2 The Services shall be performed primarily by the Key Person. The Consultant may not substitute the Key Person without the Client's prior written consent (such consent not to be unreasonably withheld).

2.3 The Consultant shall perform the Services with reasonable skill and care and in accordance with good industry practice.

## 3. STATUS

3.1 The Consultant is an independent contractor. Nothing in this Agreement creates a relationship of employer and employee, partnership, joint venture or agency between the parties.

3.2 The Consultant is responsible for accounting to HM Revenue and Customs for all income tax, National Insurance contributions and other deductions in respect of the Fees, and shall indemnify the Client against any liability arising from such payments (including penalties and interest).

3.3 The parties confirm that the IR35 status of this engagement has been assessed by the Client. A **Status Determination Statement** has been issued (Schedule 4) and concludes that the engagement falls outside Chapter 10 of Part 2 ITEPA 2003.

## 4. FEES

4.1 The Client shall pay the Consultant a fee of **£1,800 per day** (the "**Day Rate**") for time actually worked on the Services, plus reasonable pre-approved expenses.

4.2 The Consultant shall submit invoices monthly in arrears, with a timesheet itemising days worked and a summary of activities. Invoices shall be paid within 30 days.

4.3 Either party may request a review of the Day Rate annually on each anniversary of the Effective Date. If the parties cannot agree a revised Day Rate, the existing Day Rate continues unchanged.

## 5. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

5.1 All Intellectual Property Rights in any Deliverables, work product, materials or methodologies created by the Consultant in the course of providing the Services shall vest in the Client on creation. The Consultant assigns to the Client (with full title guarantee, by way of present assignment of future rights) all such Intellectual Property Rights.

5.2 The Consultant waives, and shall procure that the Key Person waives, all moral rights in the Deliverables to the fullest extent permitted by law.

5.3 The Consultant retains ownership of (a) all pre-existing material and (b) general know-how and methodologies of general application. To the extent any such material is incorporated into the Deliverables, the Consultant grants the Client a non-exclusive, perpetual, royalty-free licence to use it for the Client's business purposes.

## 6. CONFIDENTIALITY

6.1 The Consultant shall not, during or after the Engagement Period, use or disclose any Confidential Information of the Client, except in the proper course of providing the Services or as required by law.

6.2 On termination, the Consultant shall return or destroy all Confidential Information and Deliverables, save for one copy retained for record-keeping and regulatory purposes.

## 7. DATA PROTECTION

7.1 The Consultant shall comply with all Applicable Data Protection Laws in connection with any Personal Data accessed in the course of the Services. The parties shall enter into a separate data processing agreement where the Consultant Processes Personal Data on behalf of the Client.

## 8. RESTRICTIONS

8.1 During the Engagement Period and for a period of **6 months** thereafter, neither the Consultant nor the Key Person shall, directly or indirectly:

   (a) provide services materially similar to the Services to any **Restricted Competitor** (being any of the Premiership rugby clubs or any club competing in the men's first or second tier of professional rugby union in the United Kingdom);
   (b) solicit any Senior Employee of the Client with whom the Consultant or the Key Person had material dealings in the 12 months prior to termination;
   (c) solicit any Client Sponsor (as listed in Schedule 5).

8.2 The Consultant acknowledges that the restrictions in clause 8.1 are reasonable having regard to the nature of the Services and the access to confidential information they involve.

## 9. TERMINATION

9.1 Either party may terminate this Agreement on **30 days' written notice** after the initial 12-month period.

9.2 Either party may terminate immediately on written notice if the other:

   (a) commits a material breach which is incapable of remedy;
   (b) commits a material breach which is capable of remedy and fails to remedy within 14 days of written notice;
   (c) suffers an Insolvency Event;
   (d) (in the case of termination by the Client) the Key Person ceases to be able or willing to provide the Services for any reason.

9.3 The Client may terminate immediately on written notice if the Consultant or the Key Person engages in conduct which, in the Client's reasonable opinion, brings the Client into disrepute.

## 10. INSURANCE

10.1 The Consultant shall maintain throughout the Engagement Period:

   (a) Professional Indemnity insurance of not less than £2,000,000 per claim;
   (b) Public Liability insurance of not less than £5,000,000 per claim;
   (c) such other insurance as is reasonably required by the Client.

## 11. LIMITATION OF LIABILITY

11.1 Nothing in this Agreement excludes or limits either party's liability for death or personal injury caused by negligence, fraud, or any liability that cannot be limited by law. Liability under clause 3.2 (Tax Indemnity) and clause 5 (Intellectual Property) is not subject to the cap in clause 11.3.

11.2 Neither party is liable for loss of profit, revenue, business or anticipated savings, or for indirect or consequential loss.

11.3 Subject to clauses 11.1 and 11.2, the Consultant's total aggregate liability under or in connection with this Agreement is capped at **the higher of (a) £500,000 and (b) 200% of the Fees paid or payable in the 12 months preceding the event giving rise to liability**.

## 12. ENTIRE AGREEMENT

12.1 This Agreement (including its Schedules) constitutes the entire agreement between the parties relating to its subject-matter and supersedes all prior agreements and understandings.

## 13. GOVERNING LAW

13.1 This Agreement is governed by the law of England and Wales. The courts of England and Wales have exclusive jurisdiction.
