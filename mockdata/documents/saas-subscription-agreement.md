# SAAS SUBSCRIPTION AGREEMENT (extract)

**Supplier:** Lattice Networks Ltd (registered number 11203987) (the "**Supplier**")
**Customer:** Albion Retail Holdings plc (registered number 04219885) (the "**Customer**")
**Date:** 2025-12-04
**Initial Term:** 36 months from the Effective Date.
**Service:** Lattice Inventory Intelligence Platform (the "**Service**") as more particularly described in **Order Form 1**.

> *Extract of the operative terms. Order Forms, SLA Schedule, DPA and Acceptable Use Policy are referenced but omitted.*

## 1. ACCESS AND USE

1.1 Subject to payment of the Subscription Fees, the Supplier grants the Customer a non-exclusive, non-transferable, worldwide right to access and use the Service for its internal business operations during the Subscription Term, for the Authorised Users and Use Volumes specified in the Order Form.

1.2 The Customer shall not (and shall procure that its Authorised Users do not):

   (a) make the Service available to any third party except as expressly permitted;
   (b) reverse engineer, decompile or attempt to derive the source code of the Service;
   (c) use the Service to build a competing product or service;
   (d) use the Service to train, evaluate or fine-tune any machine-learning model except with the Supplier's prior written consent.

## 2. SERVICE LEVELS

2.1 The Supplier shall make the Service available with an uptime of not less than **99.9%** measured monthly, excluding Scheduled Maintenance and Excluded Events as set out in the **SLA Schedule**.

2.2 Failure to meet the Uptime Commitment entitles the Customer to Service Credits calculated as a percentage of the monthly Subscription Fee for the affected month. Service Credits are the Customer's sole financial remedy save in the case of Chronic Failure (three or more consecutive months below the Uptime Commitment), in which case the Customer may terminate under clause 11.4.

## 3. FEES AND PAYMENT

3.1 The Subscription Fees are set out in the Order Form and shall be invoiced annually in advance.

3.2 Subscription Fees may be increased at each Renewal by the greater of (a) 4% and (b) the percentage increase in the UK Consumer Prices Index over the preceding 12 months.

3.3 Overage Charges apply where the Customer's actual usage exceeds the Use Volumes specified in the Order Form, at the rates set out in the Order Form. The Supplier shall give 30 days' notice before applying Overage Charges.

3.4 All amounts are exclusive of VAT and shall be paid within 30 days of invoice.

## 4. DATA AND PRIVACY

4.1 The Customer retains all right, title and interest in the Customer Data. The Supplier acquires no rights in Customer Data other than the limited rights granted in this clause 4.

4.2 The Supplier may process Customer Data:

   (a) to provide and maintain the Service;
   (b) to produce aggregated and anonymised analytics, provided that no Authorised User or natural person is identifiable from such analytics;
   (c) where applicable law requires.

4.3 The parties acknowledge that, in the course of providing the Service, the Supplier acts as a Processor in respect of Customer Personal Data. The Data Processing Addendum at **Schedule 3** (**DPA**) governs that processing and is incorporated by reference.

## 5. SECURITY

5.1 The Supplier shall maintain a written information security programme aligned to **ISO 27001** and **SOC 2 Type II** standards. The Supplier shall provide its most recent SOC 2 Type II report to the Customer on reasonable request, not more than once in any 12-month period.

5.2 The Supplier shall notify the Customer without undue delay, and in any event within **48 hours**, on becoming aware of any Security Incident affecting Customer Data.

## 6. AI AND MODEL USE

6.1 The Service incorporates AI features (the "**AI Features**") provided by the Supplier and by third-party model providers identified in the **Subprocessor List**.

6.2 The Supplier shall not use Customer Data to train any foundation model or to improve any AI service made available to other customers. The Supplier confirms that its model providers have contractually committed to the same restriction in respect of API traffic from the Service.

6.3 The Customer acknowledges that AI Features may produce inaccurate or incomplete outputs ("**Hallucination Risk**") and agrees not to rely on AI-generated outputs without human review.

## 7. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

7.1 As between the parties, the Supplier owns the Service, all underlying software, models, documentation and Improvements. Feedback provided by the Customer may be used freely by the Supplier.

## 8. INDEMNITIES

8.1 The Supplier shall indemnify the Customer against all losses, damages and reasonable costs arising from any third-party claim that the Service or its normal use infringes that third party's Intellectual Property Rights. The Supplier may, at its option, modify the Service, procure a licence, or terminate the affected Order Form with a pro rata refund.

8.2 The Customer shall indemnify the Supplier against any third-party claim arising from Customer Data or the Customer's use of the Service in breach of clause 1.2.

## 9. LIMITATION OF LIABILITY

9.1 Nothing limits or excludes either party's liability for death or personal injury caused by negligence, fraud, or any liability that cannot be limited by law. Liability under clause 8 (Indemnities) and the DPA is not subject to the cap in clause 9.3.

9.2 Neither party is liable for loss of profit, revenue, business or anticipated savings, or for indirect or consequential loss.

9.3 Subject to clauses 9.1 and 9.2, each party's total aggregate liability in any 12-month period is capped at **150% of the Subscription Fees paid or payable in that 12-month period**.

## 10. CHANGE OF CONTROL

10.1 The Customer may terminate this Agreement on 60 days' written notice if the Supplier undergoes a Change of Control to (a) a Competitor of the Customer or (b) any entity that does not have equivalent or better information security accreditations to those held by the Supplier at the date of this Agreement.

## 11. TERM AND TERMINATION

11.1 The Initial Term is 36 months from the Effective Date. Thereafter the Agreement renews automatically for successive 12-month Renewal Terms unless either party gives 90 days' written notice prior to the end of the then-current term.

11.2 Either party may terminate immediately for the other's material breach not remedied within 30 days, or for the other's Insolvency Event.

11.3 The Customer may terminate any Order Form (without termination of the Agreement) on 60 days' notice in the event of (a) a Security Incident affecting Customer Personal Data of more than 1,000 data subjects, (b) a Change of Control under clause 10, or (c) Chronic Failure under clause 2.2.

## 12. EXIT AND DATA RETURN

12.1 On termination or expiry, the Supplier shall make Customer Data available for export in a machine-readable format for a period of **90 days**. Thereafter the Supplier shall delete or anonymise Customer Data, save to the extent required to be retained by law.

12.2 The Supplier shall provide reasonable transition assistance for up to **6 months** at its then-current professional services rates.

## 13. GOVERNING LAW

13.1 This Agreement is governed by the law of England and Wales. The courts of England and Wales have exclusive jurisdiction.
