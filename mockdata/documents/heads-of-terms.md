# HEADS OF TERMS (Project Birch)

**Buyer:** Tessera Capital Partners (acting on behalf of a portfolio company to be designated, "**BidCo**")
**Seller:** the Shareholders of Verdant DataOps Ltd ("**Verdant**" or the "**Company**")
**Date:** 2026-04-28

> *This Heads of Terms summarises the principal commercial terms of a proposed acquisition. Save for clauses 14 (Confidentiality), 15 (Exclusivity), 16 (Costs) and 17 (Governing Law), this document is **not legally binding** and is subject to contract, due diligence and Tessera Investment Committee approval.*

## 1. TRANSACTION

1.1 The Buyer proposes to acquire **100% of the issued share capital** of Verdant DataOps Ltd from the Sellers (the "**Transaction**").

1.2 The Transaction will be structured as a share purchase governed by English law, on customary PE bolt-on terms.

## 2. CONSIDERATION

2.1 The total enterprise value is **£42,500,000** (the "**Enterprise Value**"), on a **cash-free, debt-free basis**, with a **normalised working capital adjustment** by reference to a 12-month look-back average.

2.2 The Consideration will be satisfied as to:

   (a) **£34,000,000** in cash on Completion (the "**Initial Cash**");
   (b) **£4,500,000** placed into an Escrow Account on standard W&I-back-up terms for **18 months** post-Completion;
   (c) **£4,000,000** by way of **rollover equity** in the Buyer's PE vehicle, subject to standard MEP terms (leaver, vesting, ratchet).

2.3 An **earn-out** of up to **£3,500,000** payable in two equal instalments on the first and second anniversaries of Completion, contingent on EBITDA targets of £4.8m (FY27) and £5.6m (FY28) as set out in the indicative term sheet in Schedule A.

## 3. KEY ASSUMPTIONS

3.1 The Consideration is based on:

   (a) audited FY25 EBITDA of **£3.2m** and run-rate FY26 EBITDA of **£3.9m**;
   (b) Verdant having no Net Debt other than as disclosed in the Information Memorandum;
   (c) the Key Customer Contracts listed in Schedule B continuing on substantially their current terms;
   (d) retention of the Key Personnel (CEO, CTO, Head of Customer Success).

## 4. CONDITIONS PRECEDENT

4.1 Completion is conditional on:

   (a) satisfactory legal, financial, commercial and tax due diligence;
   (b) Tessera Investment Committee approval;
   (c) no Material Adverse Change between signing and Completion;
   (d) entry into new service agreements with the Key Personnel on terms acceptable to the Buyer;
   (e) consent or waivers in respect of change-of-control provisions in the Material Contracts listed in Schedule B;
   (f) approval under the National Security and Investment Act 2021, if required.

## 5. WARRANTIES AND INDEMNITIES

5.1 The Sellers will give customary commercial and tax warranties, **W&I-policy backed**.

5.2 The Sellers' liability will be capped at:

   (a) for the commercial warranties: **£1 (a nominal cap)**, with the W&I policy as the Buyer's primary recourse;
   (b) for the title and capacity warranties: **100% of Consideration received**;
   (c) for the Tax Covenant: **40% of Consideration**.

5.3 General time limits: **2 years** for commercial warranties (W&I duration); **7 years** for Tax.

5.4 De minimis: **£10,000** per claim; basket (tipping): **£150,000**.

## 6. RESTRICTIONS

6.1 The Sellers will be subject to customary non-compete and non-solicitation restrictions for **3 years** post-Completion, limited to the Restricted Territory (United Kingdom, Ireland, and the EEA states in which Verdant has clients at Completion).

## 7. EMPLOYMENT AND MEP

7.1 The CEO and CTO (the "**MEP Participants**") will enter into new service agreements with notice periods of 12 months and customary garden-leave provisions.

7.2 The MEP Participants will rollover the amounts shown in Schedule A into the Buyer's MEP, with vesting over 4 years (25% Year 1, then monthly), good/bad leaver, and a tiered ratchet linked to MoM at Exit.

## 8. FINANCING

8.1 The Buyer expects to fund the Initial Cash through a combination of equity from the Tessera Fund V and acquisition debt of approximately £15m underwritten by Northbank Markets plc (commitment letter expected w/c 11 May 2026).

8.2 The Transaction is **not subject to a financing condition**.

## BINDING PROVISIONS

The following provisions are intended to be legally binding on the parties.

## 14. CONFIDENTIALITY

14.1 The existence and contents of this Heads of Terms, the proposed Transaction, and all information disclosed by one party to the other in connection with it, are confidential and may not be disclosed save (a) to professional advisers and prospective lenders on a need-to-know basis, (b) as required by law, or (c) with the other party's prior written consent.

## 15. EXCLUSIVITY

15.1 In consideration of the Buyer incurring costs in conducting due diligence and negotiating the Transaction, the Sellers and the Company undertake that for a period of **8 weeks** from the date of this Heads of Terms (the "**Exclusivity Period**"), they will not, and will procure that no Connected Person will, directly or indirectly:

   (a) solicit, initiate or encourage any offer, proposal, enquiry or expression of interest from any third party in respect of a sale of all or any part of the share capital or business of the Company;
   (b) provide any information about the Company to any such third party;
   (c) enter into any negotiation, discussion, agreement or arrangement with any third party in respect of any such transaction.

15.2 The Sellers shall notify the Buyer promptly of any unsolicited approach.

## 16. COSTS

16.1 Each party shall bear its own costs and expenses in connection with the Transaction up to and including Completion, save that if either party walks away in breach of clauses 14 or 15, that party shall reimburse the other's external adviser fees up to a cap of £100,000.

## 17. GOVERNING LAW

17.1 This Heads of Terms (including the binding provisions) is governed by the law of England and Wales. The courts of England and Wales have exclusive jurisdiction.
