8.2 The Tax Covenant is subject to its own limitations regime in Schedule 8.

## 9. LIMITATIONS ON CLAIMS

9.1 **Cap on Sellers' liability**

(a) The maximum aggregate liability of the Sellers in respect of all Claims shall not exceed:

   (i) in respect of claims for breach of the Fundamental Warranties: 100% of the Consideration;
   (ii) in respect of claims under the Tax Covenant: 100% of the Consideration;
   (iii) in respect of claims under the Specific Indemnities: 100% of the Consideration;
   (iv) in respect of all other Claims: 50% of the Consideration.

> *Buyer's mark-up: clause 9.1(a)(iv) amended from 50% to 100%. Buyer's marginal note: "Standard for our deals is 100% subject to disclosure. Will accept 75% with W&I underpinning the gap."*
>
> *Internal note for Marcus: G says push back to 50%, accept 75% if buyer pushes hard. Reflect in mark-up.*

(b) Nothing in this clause limits liability for fraud or fraudulent misrepresentation.

9.2 **Time limits**

A Claim must be notified to the Sellers in writing:

   (a) in respect of the Tax Warranties and Tax Covenant: within 7 years after Completion;
   (b) in respect of the Fundamental Warranties: within 5 years after Completion;
   (c) in respect of all other Warranties and Specific Indemnities: within 24 months after Completion.

> *Buyer's mark-up: 24 months changed to 36 months. Internal note: 24 is market for this size deal; we should resist.*

9.3 **De minimis and threshold**

(a) The Sellers shall have no liability for any individual Claim where the amount of the Claim is less than **£25,000** (the "**De Minimis**").

(b) The Sellers shall have no liability for any Claim until the aggregate amount of all Claims (excluding Claims below the De Minimis) exceeds **£200,000** (the "**Threshold**"), at which point the Sellers shall be liable for the whole amount and not just the excess.

> *Buyer's mark-up: tipping basket struck through; Buyer wants excess basket only. Internal note: tipping baskets are market-standard at this size. Hold the line.*

9.4 **Conduct of claims**

If a Claim arises by reason of a third-party claim, the Buyer shall:

   (a) give the Sellers written notice as soon as reasonably practicable;
   (b) consult with the Sellers regarding the conduct of the third-party claim;
   (c) not settle or compromise the third-party claim without the Sellers' prior written consent (such consent not to be unreasonably withheld).

9.5 **No double recovery**

The Buyer shall not be entitled to recover more than once in respect of any single loss.

9.6 **Insurance recoveries**

The amount of any Claim shall be reduced by any sum actually recovered by any Group Company under any insurance policy.

9.7 **Disclosure**

The Sellers shall not be liable in respect of any Claim if and to the extent that the matter giving rise to the Claim is fairly disclosed in the Disclosure Letter (and, in this Agreement as drafted, the Data Room - though see clause 6.2(b) above and Buyer's mark-up).

## 10. WARRANTY AND INDEMNITY INSURANCE

10.1 The Buyer has procured a buy-side W&I insurance policy with [Insurer] of an aggregate amount of £35,000,000 (the "**W&I Policy**").

10.2 Save for claims under the Specific Indemnities, the Tax Covenant or claims arising from fraud, the Buyer shall first seek to recover under the W&I Policy before bringing a Claim against the Sellers.

10.3 The Sellers' aggregate liability for breach of the Warranties (other than Fundamental Warranties) shall be capped at the lesser of (a) the cap under clause 9.1; and (b) the W&I retention plus £1,000,000.
