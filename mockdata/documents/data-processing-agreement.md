# DATA PROCESSING AGREEMENT

**Controller:** Brindle Foods plc (registered number 03871924) (the "**Controller**")
**Processor:** Pebble Analytics Ltd (registered number 12889774) (the "**Processor**")
**Date:** 2026-01-22

> *Standalone DPA entered into in connection with the Cookie Consent and Customer Analytics Services Agreement between the parties of even date. The Services involve processing of Personal Data of the Controller's website visitors and loyalty programme members.*

## 1. INTERPRETATION

1.1 Capitalised terms used in this DPA have the meanings given in the **UK GDPR** and the **Data Protection Act 2018**, except where otherwise defined.

1.2 In this DPA:

   "**Applicable Data Protection Laws**" means the UK GDPR, the Data Protection Act 2018, the Privacy and Electronic Communications (EC Directive) Regulations 2003, and any successor or replacement legislation.

   "**Services**" means the services provided by the Processor to the Controller under the Services Agreement.

   "**Sub-processor**" means any third party engaged by the Processor (or any Sub-processor) to Process Personal Data on its behalf.

## 2. SUBJECT-MATTER AND DURATION

2.1 The Processor shall Process Personal Data only for the duration of the Services Agreement and only for the purposes set out in **Annex A (Processing Details)**, which describes:

   (a) the subject-matter and duration of the Processing;
   (b) the nature and purpose of the Processing;
   (c) the type of Personal Data and categories of Data Subjects;
   (d) the obligations and rights of the Controller.

## 3. PROCESSOR OBLIGATIONS

3.1 The Processor shall:

   (a) **Documented Instructions.** Process Personal Data only on documented instructions from the Controller, including transfers outside the UK. The Services Agreement and this DPA constitute the Controller's documented instructions. If the Processor is required by law to Process Personal Data otherwise, it shall notify the Controller before such Processing unless the law prohibits such notice.

   (b) **Confidentiality.** Ensure that persons authorised to Process Personal Data are bound by appropriate obligations of confidentiality.

   (c) **Security.** Implement the technical and organisational measures set out in **Annex B (Security Measures)** to ensure a level of security appropriate to the risk, including:
      - pseudonymisation and encryption of Personal Data in transit and at rest (AES-256, TLS 1.2 or above);
      - ability to ensure ongoing confidentiality, integrity, availability and resilience;
      - ability to restore availability and access in a timely manner;
      - regular testing and evaluation.

   (d) **Sub-processors.** Comply with clause 5.

   (e) **Data Subject Rights.** Assist the Controller, by appropriate technical and organisational measures, in fulfilling the Controller's obligations to respond to Data Subject requests. The Processor shall notify the Controller within **48 hours** of receiving any communication from a Data Subject and shall not respond except on the Controller's instructions.

   (f) **Article 32-36 Assistance.** Assist the Controller with security, breach notification, DPIAs and prior consultation, taking into account the nature of the Processing and the information available.

   (g) **Return and Deletion.** At the Controller's choice on termination, return or delete all Personal Data and copies, unless retention is required by law.

   (h) **Audit and Information.** Make available all information necessary to demonstrate compliance with this DPA and allow for, and contribute to, audits conducted by the Controller or an auditor mandated by the Controller, on **30 days' written notice**, not more than once in any 12-month period (save where required by a Supervisory Authority).

## 4. SECURITY INCIDENTS

4.1 The Processor shall notify the Controller without undue delay, and in any event within **24 hours**, on becoming aware of a Personal Data Breach.

4.2 The notification shall contain (or, where not all information is available, shall be supplemented as soon as practicable with):

   (a) a description of the nature of the breach, including categories and approximate numbers of Data Subjects and Personal Data records;
   (b) the name and contact details of the Processor's data protection point of contact;
   (c) likely consequences;
   (d) measures taken or proposed to address the breach and mitigate its effects.

4.3 The Processor shall not make any public announcement or notification to Data Subjects or Supervisory Authorities about the breach without the Controller's prior written consent, save as required by law.

## 5. SUB-PROCESSORS

5.1 The Processor has the Controller's general written authorisation for the engagement of Sub-processors listed in **Annex C (Approved Sub-processors)**.

5.2 The Processor shall give the Controller **not less than 30 days' prior written notice** before engaging any new Sub-processor or replacing an existing Sub-processor. The Controller may object on reasonable grounds (including grounds related to data protection compliance). If the parties cannot agree a solution within 14 days, the Controller may terminate the Services Agreement or the affected portion of it on 30 days' notice, without penalty.

5.3 The Processor shall impose on each Sub-processor data protection obligations no less protective than those imposed on the Processor under this DPA, and remains fully liable for any failure by a Sub-processor.

## 6. INTERNATIONAL TRANSFERS

6.1 The Processor shall not transfer Personal Data outside the UK except (a) to a country in respect of which there is a UK adequacy regulation, or (b) where the transfer is made pursuant to:

   (a) the **UK International Data Transfer Agreement (IDTA)**;
   (b) the **UK Addendum to the EU Standard Contractual Clauses**; or
   (c) another appropriate safeguard under Article 46 UK GDPR.

6.2 Where Personal Data is transferred to a Sub-processor in a third country, the Processor shall conduct (and, on request, share with the Controller) a Transfer Risk Assessment evaluating the laws and practices of the destination country.

## 7. AI AND AUTOMATED PROCESSING

7.1 The Processor shall not (and shall procure that no Sub-processor shall) use Personal Data to train, evaluate or fine-tune any machine-learning or AI model, save where the model is used solely for the purposes of providing the Services to the Controller and the training data is segregated from data of other customers.

7.2 The Processor shall provide the Controller with such information as the Controller reasonably requests in connection with any AI Impact Assessment under Article 35 UK GDPR.

## 8. LIABILITY

8.1 Each party's liability under this DPA shall be subject to the limitations of liability in the Services Agreement, save that nothing in the Services Agreement excludes or limits a party's liability for (a) breach of clause 6 (International Transfers), (b) breach of clause 7 (AI), or (c) statutory liability under Article 82 UK GDPR.

## 9. PRECEDENCE

9.1 In the event of any conflict between this DPA and the Services Agreement, this DPA prevails.

## 10. GOVERNING LAW

10.1 This DPA is governed by the law of England and Wales. The courts of England and Wales have exclusive jurisdiction.

## ANNEX A - PROCESSING DETAILS (summary)

**Subject-matter:** cookie consent management; analytics on customer interactions with Brindle websites and mobile apps; loyalty programme analytics.

**Duration:** the Term of the Services Agreement plus 90 days for export and deletion.

**Nature and purpose:** collection of consent signals; aggregation of behavioural events; production of cohort and journey analytics; segmentation for marketing.

**Categories of Data Subject:** website visitors, mobile-app users, loyalty members.

**Categories of Personal Data:** online identifiers, device IDs, IP address (truncated), behavioural events, loyalty number, postcode, marketing preferences. **No Special Category Data.**

## ANNEX B - SECURITY MEASURES (summary)

Pseudonymisation; encryption in transit and at rest (TLS 1.2+, AES-256); MFA; role-based access controls; SOC 2 Type II; quarterly penetration testing; documented incident response procedure with 24-hour SLA; annual employee data protection training.
