# CONTRACT OF EMPLOYMENT

**THIS AGREEMENT** is made on **2025-09-08**

**BETWEEN:**

**(1) BRINDLE FOODS PLC** (registered number 03871924) whose registered office is at Brindle House, Wokingham Road, Reading RG6 7HE (the "**Company**"); and

**(2) ALEX MORDEN** of [address on file] (the "**Employee**").

## 1. APPOINTMENT

1.1 The Company appoints the Employee as **Head of Procurement** reporting to the Chief Operating Officer.

1.2 Employment commenced on **6 October 2025**. No previous employment counts as continuous employment.

## 2. PROBATION

2.1 The first six months of employment shall be a probationary period. During this period, either party may terminate on one week's written notice. The probation period may be extended by up to three months at the Company's discretion.

## 3. PLACE OF WORK

3.1 The Employee's normal place of work is the Company's Reading head office. The Employee may be required to travel to other Company locations and to suppliers' premises.

3.2 The Company offers hybrid working: a minimum of three days per week in the Reading office. The Company reserves the right to amend this policy on reasonable notice.

## 4. HOURS

4.1 Normal working hours are 9.00am to 5.30pm Monday to Friday with a one-hour unpaid lunch break, but the Employee is expected to work such hours as are reasonably necessary for the proper performance of duties.

4.2 The Employee agrees that the limit on working hours in regulation 4(1) of the Working Time Regulations 1998 shall not apply. Either party may give three months' written notice to bring this opt-out to an end.

## 5. SALARY AND BENEFITS

5.1 The Employee's salary is **£92,000 per annum** payable monthly in arrears.

5.2 The Employee is eligible to participate in the Company's discretionary annual bonus scheme. The on-target bonus is 25% of base salary. Bonus is conditional on (a) the Employee being employed and not under notice on the payment date and (b) Company and individual performance conditions.

5.3 The Employee is eligible to:

   (a) join the Company's defined-contribution pension scheme (employer contribution 8%, employee minimum 4%);
   (b) participate in the Company's Long-Term Incentive Plan, subject to award terms;
   (c) private medical insurance for the Employee, partner and dependent children;
   (d) life assurance (4x base salary);
   (e) 27 days' holiday plus public holidays.

## 6. SICKNESS

6.1 The Company shall pay full salary and benefits during periods of certified sickness for up to 13 weeks in any rolling 12-month period, after which Statutory Sick Pay applies.

## 7. CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

7.1 The Employee shall not, during or after employment, use or disclose any Confidential Information of the Company or any client, supplier or business contact, except in the proper course of duties or as required by law.

## 8. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

8.1 All Intellectual Property Rights in any work produced by the Employee in the course of employment shall belong to the Company. The Employee waives all moral rights to the fullest extent permitted by law.

## 9. TERMINATION AND NOTICE

9.1 After probation, either party may terminate this Agreement on **six months' written notice**.

9.2 The Company may make a payment in lieu of notice equal to the Employee's basic salary for the unworked portion of the notice period, plus contractual benefits as set out in this Agreement.

9.3 The Company may terminate without notice for gross misconduct.

## 10. GARDEN LEAVE

10.1 During any notice period (whether given by the Company or the Employee), the Company may require the Employee to remain at home, not contact clients, suppliers or employees, return Company property, and not commence employment elsewhere.

## 11. POST-TERMINATION RESTRICTIONS

11.1 The Employee shall not, for a period of **12 months** following the Termination Date, either alone or in conjunction with any other person, directly or indirectly:

   (a) be engaged in or concerned with any business which competes with the Restricted Business in the Restricted Territory;
   (b) solicit or seek to solicit any client of the Company with whom the Employee had material dealings in the 12 months prior to termination;
   (c) solicit or seek to solicit any senior employee of the Company.

11.2 "**Restricted Business**" means the procurement, distribution or wholesale supply of frozen and chilled food products to UK retailers. "**Restricted Territory**" means the United Kingdom.

11.3 The Employee acknowledges that these restrictions are reasonable having regard to the Employee's seniority and access to confidential information.

## 12. DATA PROTECTION

12.1 The Company will process the Employee's personal data in accordance with its Privacy Notice for Employees (a copy of which is available on the intranet).

## 13. GRIEVANCE AND DISCIPLINARY

13.1 The Company's Grievance Policy and Disciplinary Policy apply (intranet). These policies do not form part of this Agreement.

## 14. ENTIRE AGREEMENT

14.1 This Agreement, together with the Schedule, constitutes the entire agreement between the parties and supersedes all prior agreements, representations and understandings.

## 15. GOVERNING LAW

15.1 This Agreement shall be governed by the law of England and Wales. The parties submit to the exclusive jurisdiction of the courts of England and Wales.
